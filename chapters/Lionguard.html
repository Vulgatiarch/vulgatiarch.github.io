Space Marine Chapter

The Lionguard

Overview

The Lionguard are an Eleventh Founding Chapter of the Dark Angels. The founding members of the Chapter hail from the Angels of Absolution Unforgiven successor chapter. In the year 321.M37, Warp Storm Dionys would ravage their homeworld, and they would become one of the Judged to undertake the Abyssal Crusade. It is during this Crusade that they would become the Renegade Chapter known as the Death Shadows.[1][2]

History

The Nova Terra Interregnum

The Lionguard were born in the Eleventh Founding, immediately following the Nova Terra Interregnum. Following the Interregnum, the High Lords of Terra sought to establish chapters in Segmentum Pacificus to act as rapid response forces should voices of secession or heresy rise once again in the region. This resulted in many Chapters of the Eleventh Founding of late M35 being stationed within Segmentum Pacificus.

The founding members of the Lionguard hail from of the 2nd Company of the Angels of Absolution Chapter, with the highly-mobile, fast-attack tactics of the 2nd Company being favored for a rapid-response force. At this point, the Chapter was primarily fleet-based, pulling its recruits from Shrine Worlds in the Segmentum that had proved instrumental in overthrowing the Ur-Council. Feudal Worlds with strong webs of fealty and cultures of oaths were also used to draw new Aspirants, with the idea that strong cultures of loyalty would bolster the Chapter's resiliency against the temptations of the traitorous forces they warded against.

One such world was the Feudal World of Surdlande, which the Chapter ultimately established as its formal homeworld. The world was tidally locked with its sun, allowing the establishment of a fortress on the dark side of the planet with a natural barrier with collosal fields of ice wastes providing a natural barrier against the Surders, and a proving ground for future Aspirants.

The Abyssal Crusade

Following the Ecclesiarchal Purges of 321.M37, a dozen star systems were engulfed by Warp Storm Dionys, its echoes rippling along the spiral arms of the galaxy as it raged through the Immaterium. Records of mutation and Chaos Cultist activity quadrupled overnight. Worse yet, it was not only the citizens who were affected by the sudden influx of Chaos. Many of the Space Marine Chapters with homeworlds affected by the Warp Storm found that the imperfections lying within their gene-seed were writ large upon their new recruits, giving rise to a wave of distrubing manifestations both physical and psychological. The Lionguard were one such Loyalist Chapter of the Adeptus Astartes that had been affected by these Warp Storms.[2]

On their homeworld of Surdlande, the effects of the Warp Storm had caused a heightened rate of a condition suffered by the Chapter that had been termed "The Hollow." The result of neurological degredation caused by the excess of aluminum in Surdlande's environemnt, the Hollow left the minds of many of the Lionguard present vulnerable to assault by the denizens of the Immaterium. Possession wracked the Companies of the Chapter present at the time, the 1st, 6th and 10th Companies. 6th Company felt the brunt of this, and most significantly lost was the Master Librarian Germungaap.

When the Ecclesiarchy heard of this sinister turn of events, Saint Basillius the Elder demanded that all Chapters of the Adeptus Astartes whose homeworlds had been touched by the Warp Storm be rendered unto his judgement. Such was the elder's influence with the High Lords of Terra that within a standard year, this had come to pass. After a series of stringent tests and prognostications, hundreds of chapters were deemed unaffected by the Warp Storm.[2]

No less than thirty were found wanting. The Judged, as these fallen Chapters came to be known, volunteered for a redemptive penitent crusade. The most militant of their number demanded the right to purify their tainted flesh in the fires of battle, to make a noble end from tragic misfortune. To the surprise of his closest advisors, Saint Basillius agreed to this proposal. He saw it fitting to send the accused into the Eye of Terror, taking the fight for the Imperium's future to teh Daemon Worlds inhabited by the Traitor Marines.[2]

A representative from each of the doomed Chapters held an emergency Council of Dismay to discuss the proposed Crusasde. After scant solar hours of debate, they acquiesced to Basillius' demands, for they believed that martyrdom was preferable to an existencec of suspicion and doubt.[2]

The last few solar days of 321.M37 saw a solemn procession of strike cruisers and battle barges pass through the Cadian Gate into the Eye of Terror, relay systems dormant and heraldic colours obscured by black mag-plates. [2]

One by one, the Chapters of The Judged disappeared into the iridescent dust nebulae that surrounded the Eye. As the massive Space Marine flotilla entered the Eye of Terror, they were set upon by an equally large Chaos warfleet. The resultant battle was so fierce that the ships of The Judged were forced to retreat and were scattered to the furthest corners of the Eye. [2]

Culture

The Lionguard place a significant emphasis on the importance of oaths, stemming from their founding purpose to stamp out traitors and renegades within the Segmentum Pacificus. Members of the chapter are drawn from the noble houses of Surdlande. From the time they become Aspirants, they forsake their family names, and take on oath to the Chapter and Emperor of Mankind, whom the people of Surdlande identify as the Liege of Lieges. These oaths are recorded by an Oathkeeper, a special type of Chaplain the chapter utilizes to keep record of the many oaths taken by members of the Chapter.

Combat Doctrine and Organization

Having been drawn originally from the 2nd Company of the Angels of Absolution, and acting primarily as a rapid-response force, the Lionguard place a heavy emphasis on the use of armored and mechanized tactics. The Chapter holds a disproportionate number of Rhinos, Predators, and Razorbacks in its armory. These vehicles are not assigned to a given company, but instead are assigned as needed. Many Astartes end up growing somewhat attached to their gear, and as such are given the opportunity to claim a tank or bike as their own, should they perform a great deed with it.

The 1st Company of the Lionguard also bears the name "Crownwing." This is the Chapter's equivalent of the Unforgiven Deathwing, with the added caveat that induction into the 1st Company requires induction into the Order of Shattered Crowns. Induction into the Order requires the successful elimination of high-priority targets in duels, with the bearing of the duel being fair or with odds against the Astartes who seeks to join. Crownwing does have access to the terminator armor typically associated with the 1st Company of an Unforgiven chapter.

The 2nd Company of the Lionguard bears the name "Ironwing." It is unknown if this company has any real connection to the wing of the Hexagrammaton of the same name. In isolated deployments, the Ironwing are known for moving rapidly through enemy lines, bringing a heavy armored force behind enemy lines to wreak havoc before command knows the line has faltered. In joint deployment with other forces, the 2nd Company engages typical "blitzkrieg" style assaults, punching through enemy lines while supporting forces form a fortified supply line in their wake.

The remaining companies of the Lionguard are primary Codex-compliant in their structure, with the exception of 7th and 8th company. While standard Codex-compliant Chapters hold the 7th Company as a second Tactical Squad Reserve Company, The tactics of the Lionguard use the 7th Company as a second Close Assault Company. The roles of a standard 8th Company are split among the 7th and 8th Companies of the Lionguard, with 7th Company favoring bikes, and 8th Company favoring jump packs.

Recruitment

Prior to establishing the planet of Surdlande as their homeworld, the Lionguard drew their Aspirants from the Shrine and Feudal Worlds across the Segmentum Pacificus, prioritizing those worlds which brought the end of the Ur-Council, or that held heavily oath-centric cultures.

Following the settlement of Surdlande, Aspirants are drawn almost exclusively from the children of the noble houses of the planet. Would-be Aspirants most often prove themselves by hunting the great megafauna of the planet, however special invitations can be extended to those who have proven themselves through other means. Aspirants are then made to engage in a public spectacle of combat at the sacred ground of the planet where the Lionguard first touched down on the planet. The fighting only stops when the Master Chaplain overseeing the trial decrees. Following this combat, those Aspirants who remain take oaths, forsaking their family names, and are sent into the ice wastes to find their way to the Chapter's fortress. During this time, Aspirants are expected to rely on themselves, and it is seen as an opportunity to prove that old house rivalries have been put aside among these new brothers.

Those who make it to the walls of the fortress have proven themselves worthy to become full brothers of the Astartes. After becoming Scout Marines, brothers are sent back out into the wastes. Now, these brothers are expected to explore the ruins of Surdlande's long-lost past. No records of this period exist, but great ruins suggest a large civilization in a state of development mirroring the post-Medieval era of Terra's ancient past. Throughout these wastes, many monuments and memorials have been possessed by daemons of the Warp. In order to graduate from the Scouts, Astartes are expected to excise one such daemon, in the process learning its name. This name is not the daemons true name, but a name they will take as a trophy, using it in place of their old family name.

Heraldry

The badge of the Lionguard is a white lion rampant, with a crown adorning its head.

The Lionguard's armor is a stark metallic, reminiscent of the metallic form of the aluminum that is so integral to Surdlande's environment. Their pauldrons are painted a vibrant red. Squad iconography is painted in white, with squad numbers painted in black.

Company markigns are noted according to the markings of the Dark Angels, however with their metallic armor replacing green, and red replacing bone.

Crownwing's armor stands out among their brethren, as they bear striking golden trim and golden helmets.
